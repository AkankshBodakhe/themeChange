Tesseract

A tesseract also known as a hypercube, is a four-dimensional geometric shape that serves as the 4D analog of a cube. Just as a cube is an extension of a square into the third dimension, a tesseract extends a cube into the fourth spatial dimension, which is beyond the three dimensions we experience in everyday life. Structurally, a tesseract consists of 8 cubical cells, 16 vertices, 32 edges, and 24 square faces, with each edge of equal length. Since humans cannot directly visualize four-dimensional objects, tesseracts are often represented as 3D projections or 2D diagrams, commonly depicted as two cubes connected at their vertices.

The tesseract holds significant importance in various fields. In mathematics, it is studied in higher-dimensional geometry and topology, helping to explore the properties of spaces with more than three dimensions. In physics, it plays a role in theories involving additional spatial dimensions, such as string theory and advanced cosmological models. Computer science uses tesseracts in data visualization and graphical representations of multi-dimensional data. Additionally, the tesseract has gained prominence in popular culture, often symbolizing advanced technology or higher-dimensional concepts, such as its depiction in the movie Interstellar and its role as a powerful artifact in the Marvel Cinematic Universe. While the tesseract may appear as a "cube within a cube" in projections, this is simply a visual analogy for its 4D nature rather than an actual containment of cubes.
